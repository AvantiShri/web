I graduated from MIT in 2013 with a degree in Computational Biology, and spent a productive year working for the Healthcare team of Palantir Technologies before beginning my PhD at Stanford. I spent the first 18 years of my life in Bangalore, India, but if you compliment me with "your English is very good", you will get the response "thanks; so is yours".

If there's one thing I'm uncommonly passionate about, it's improving the quality of political discourse. I find it absurd that we tend to think in terms of "debates", where there is a "side" that "wins"; it leads to sloppy reasoning that people would easily see as flawed if they didn't feel so threatened by disagreement. You can find more of my thoughts on my facebook feed. I am particularly proud of this post.
